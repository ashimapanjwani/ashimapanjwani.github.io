Hey there! I’m a software developer with a degree in pharmacy.
My programming journey began in college when I started doing MOOCs in Biostatistics and Bioinformatics. I was introduced to R and Python via these courses. Having thoroughly enjoyed learning these languages, someone suggested that I should maybe try my hand at learning web development. So I started doing a Specialization on Coursera by Prof. Colleen van Lent called "Web Design for Everybody" (Basics of Web Development and Coding). Meanwhile, I also started applying for internships at various tech startups since I knew that I would learn much more quickly from real life experience than by doing a course. I finally landed an internship at a travel technology company (my current workplace) where I helped build a Client Relationship Management(CRM) Software using the LAMP stack. Having never used PHP before, it was a very exciting, exhausting but gratifying experience to learn the language in such a short span of time. This experience also reinforced my desire to excel at programming. Nowadays I spend most of my time either learning new skills or polishing the old ones.
In my free time, I enjoy reading and writing. This website is a commonplace book where I share what I’ve learnt, my thoughts on different issues, and other cool findings that I may have come across (hence the name ‘Findings, Lessons and Thoughts’).
